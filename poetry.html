Poetry

I love words arranged with intention. Words that breathe, and communicate identity, place, struggle, wonder — and the quiet spaces between certainty and doubt.

On Writing Poetry

In poetry , every word has to earn its place. You have to be honest and vulnerable. Every line break or comma is a decision. The compression it demands forces a kind of clarity that other forms allow you to avoid.

The poems here move between the personal and the poetry of imagination, All intimate and not forced. They are fragements. All of them are attempts to say something true, in the fewest words possible.

Struggle

Ghouls

You know who they are
I.
The light itself turned traitor casting menacing shadows His mind was somewhere else Or maybe it was here, but he wasn’t Like ashes of a forgotten memory Where his reality mixed with hellish hate and spoiled goods Fear came. It didn’t knock It sat down like it lived here He tried to stop his heart from breaking out of his chest Tried to fight it the way you try to fight the rain or the dry season It took him anyway Took him to a place Where nothing made sense And his soul was gambled away with dice In cruel treaties with fate...
A meditation written more than a decade ago, that still reasonates. It asks no questions
Life

The Change

A celebration
II.
With delicious cries, she burst upon the world As pain evaporated Leaving only the sweet ecstasy known by mothers Mother's tears washed the small body, a first bath And the sun beat his chest with pride from his high seat For illuminating the stage of this first breath All the nurses bubbled with laughter And the midwife swelled with pride The nosy breeze could not be kept away And arrived to give a jubilant slap on the bottom The father stood clumsily on the edge Trembling with excitement and surprise With another glance, he saw his wife as if for the first time And now, the world had changed Changed foreve.
Written upon the birth of my niece
Place

My City

On home
III.
I come from the unrelenting sun Ilanga leNketa, as if it burns with a secret only we know I am from wide, wide roads that stretch their arms wide across the land Where the entire road belongs to me when I walk From sparse earth Full of swamps and dry savannah grass I come from streets that dance Where music tumbles from speakers on Verandahs And laughter spills into the streets The pastime for the youth is to scatter hope and joy with their quick feet I was born beneath the memory of industry A cloud heavy with steel and dreams Before greedy men with full bellies and fat hands swallowed the land Leaving hollow skeletons Warehouses - where once the heart of the city beat As if cursing us for daring to hope I am from oppression and blood....
A poem about the city that birthed me. THis is a place where a great deal of who I am was formed. It lives in my psyche
