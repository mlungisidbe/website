Why Read History?

History does not repeat. But it rhymes, persistently and with variation, in ways that reward careful attention. The political crises, technological disruptions, moral failures, and unexpected triumphs that define our age all have precedents — not identical ones, but close enough to illuminate the patterns that drive human affairs.

Books are how those patterns get transmitted. Not just history books, but novels, philosophy, biography, and science. The best books slow you down, force you to inhabit unfamiliar minds, and leave you less certain and more curious than when you started. That is not a failure of understanding. It is its beginning.

"The past is never dead. It's not even past." — William Faulkner
