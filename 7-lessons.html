7 Lessons to My
Younger Self.

Mlungisi Dube
Technology Risk & Assurance
7 Lessons

Starting a career in Technology Risk and Assurance can be exciting or confusing. Or a combination. Here are seven things I wish someone had told me. Lessons learned the slow, sometimes painful way — for anyone beginning that journey.

7 Lessons
01
Knowledge

Focus on acquiring knowledge and skills over money.

If you must choose between two offers, choose the one with a learning culture. You won't regret it. The money will come — the compounding value of skills and knowledge acquired early in your career will outpace any short-term salary advantage many times over.

02
Growth

Put yourself in uncomfortable situations.

You gain more confidence by proving to yourself that you can do what you doubt you can do. That will mean failures and pain along the way — but it also means growth. If you are afraid of speaking in public, the solution is to talk in public more. Comfort is the enemy of capability.

03
Leadership

Choose the right manager.

They often hold the key to your work environment and your progression. If they challenge, support and are honest — stay. If you are just a number on the resource chart and they show no interest in you, look for another manager or job. A great manager is worth more than a great title.

04
Relationships

Relationships Matter!

A scary proposition for introverts like me, but work, like life, is richer when you cultivate valuable relationships. We are all figuring it out and we need each other's help. The people you build genuine connections with will open doors, share knowledge, and stand by you in ways no job title ever will.

05
Communication

Learn to speak and write well.

You will collaborate often. Most of it will happen in busy environments with limited time. The better you communicate — the harder it is to dismiss you and your ideas. In a field built on evidence and findings, the ability to express complex risk clearly and concisely is one of the most underrated competitive advantages you can develop.

06
Advocacy

No one is coming to save you.

Putting your head down and focusing on work ONLY isn't the brilliant idea you think it is. If you don't learn to advocate for yourself and your work, no one will come and dig you from the hole you are hiding in. Unlike in movies where the mantra is — show, don't tell — you need to show and tell. Make your work visible. Own your narrative.

07
Purpose

Help as many people as you can along the way!

This is the one that ties all the others together. The knowledge you accumulate, the relationships you build, the voice you develop — use them generously. A career in risk and assurance is ultimately about protecting people and organisations. Extend that spirit of protection to the people around you too.

A Final Word
No map exists for this career. But if you stay curious, stay uncomfortable, and stay generous — you will find your way. And you will help others find theirs.
